## 1. Getting Started

### 1.1 Accessing the Admin Panel

1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge recommended)
2. Type the admin URL in the address bar: `https://apexautomotive.co.uk/admin`
3. Press Enter
4. The login screen will appear

**Bookmark tip:** Add the admin page to your browser bookmarks for quick access.

### 1.2 Logging In

1. Enter your **email address** in the first field
2. Enter your **password** in the second field
3. Click the **Sign In** button
4. If you have two-factor authentication enabled, enter the code from your phone
5. You will be taken to the Dashboard

**Troubleshooting login issues:**

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| "Invalid credentials" | Check your email and password. Passwords are case-sensitive. |
| "Account locked" | Contact your administrator to unlock your account |
| "Session expired" | Log in again — sessions expire after 24 hours of inactivity |
| Page won't load | Check your internet connection and try again |

### 1.3 Dashboard Overview

After logging in, you will see the **Dashboard** page:

**Top Navigation Bar:**
- **Search icon** — Quick search across the system
- **Notification bell** — Alerts for new leads, assignments, etc.
- **Your profile photo** — Click to access your account settings or log out

**Left Sidebar Menu:**
- Dashboard
- Vehicles
- Leads
- Analytics (Admin only)
- Settings (Admin only)

**Main Content Area:**
- **KPI Cards** — Quick stats showing vehicles in stock, new leads, test drives booked, and conversion rate
- **Sales Chart** — Visual chart of sales over the last 12 months
- **Lead Funnel** — Shows how leads progress through your sales pipeline
- **Recent Activity** — Latest actions by all team members

### 1.4 Your Profile

To update your profile:

1. Click your **profile photo** in the top-right corner
2. Select **"My Profile"** from the dropdown
3. Update your information:
   - **First Name** and **Last Name**
   - **Phone Number**
   - **Profile Photo** — Click to upload a new photo
   - **Password** — Click "Change Password" to update
4. Click **"Save Changes"**

## 2. Managing Vehicles

The Vehicles section is where you add, edit, and manage all vehicles in your inventory.

### 2.1 Viewing Your Vehicle List

1. Click **Vehicles** in the left sidebar
2. You will see a table listing all vehicles

**Understanding the vehicle table:**

| Column | What It Shows |
|--------|--------------|
| Image | Primary photo of the vehicle |
| Make & Model | Vehicle manufacturer and model name |
| Year | Year of registration |
| Price | Advertised sale price |
| Status | Current availability status |
| Featured | Whether shown on homepage |
| Views | Number of times viewed on website |
| Actions | Buttons to view, edit, or delete |

**Sorting:** Click any column header to sort by that column. Click again to reverse the order.

**Searching:** Type in the search box to find vehicles by make, model, or registration number.

### 2.2 Adding a New Vehicle

Follow these steps to add a vehicle to your inventory:

#### Step 1: Open the Add Vehicle Form

1. Click the **"Add Vehicle"** button in the top-right corner
2. The vehicle creation form will open

#### Step 2: Basic Information

Fill in the vehicle details:

| Field | What to Enter | Example |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| Make | Vehicle manufacturer | BMW, Mercedes, Audi |
| Model | Vehicle model name | 3 Series, A-Class, A4 |
| Year | Year of first registration | 2022 |
| Variant | Trim level or edition | M Sport, AMG Line, S Line |
| Registration | UK registration plate | BF22 ABC |

**Tip:** If you enter the registration number and click **Lookup**, the system may auto-fill some details (requires DVLA lookup service).

#### Step 3: Specifications

Complete the technical specifications:

| Field | Options |
|-------|---------|
| Body Type | Saloon, Hatchback, Estate, SUV, Coupe, Convertible, MPV, Pickup, Van |
| Fuel Type | Petrol, Diesel, Hybrid, Electric |
| Transmission | Manual, Automatic |
| Engine Size | e.g., "2.0L", "3.0L TDI" |
| Doors | Number of doors |
| Seats | Number of seats |
| Colour | Exterior colour |
| Mileage | Current odometer reading |
| Condition | Brief description of condition |

#### Step 4: Pricing

Enter pricing details:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Cash Price | The price customers pay to buy outright |
| Finance Price | Price when purchasing on finance (if different) |
| Monthly Payment | Representative monthly payment amount |
| APR | Annual Percentage Rate for finance |
| Deposit | Required deposit amount |

#### Step 5: Features

Select all features that apply:

1. You will see a grid of feature checkboxes
2. Click each feature the vehicle has
3. Selected features will be highlighted
4. Click again to deselect

**Common features include:** Satellite Navigation, Leather Seats, Parking Sensors, Reverse Camera, Bluetooth, Climate Control, Cruise Control, LED Headlights, Alloy Wheels, Apple CarPlay.

#### Step 6: Description

Write a detailed description:

1. Click in the **Description** text area
2. Write at least 150 words
3. Include: key selling points, service history, condition details, notable features
4. Use paragraphs for readability

**Writing tips:**
- Start with a strong opening line highlighting the vehicle's best features
- Mention service history and number of previous owners
- List the most impressive features
- End with a call to action ("Viewing recommended," "Finance available")

#### Step 7: Upload Images

1. Scroll to the **Images** section
2. Click the upload area or drag and drop image files from your computer
3. Images will upload automatically
4. Drag images to reorder them
5. Click the **star icon** on an image to set it as the primary (main) image
6. Click the **trash icon** to remove an image

**Image requirements:**
- Format: JPG, PNG, or WebP
- Maximum size: 10MB per image
- Maximum count: 20 images
- Recommended minimum resolution: 1920x1080 pixels

**Recommended shots:**
1. Front exterior
2. Rear exterior
3. Side profile
4. Interior dashboard
5. Interior seats (front)
6. Interior seats (rear)
7. Engine bay
8. Boot/trunk
9. Key features (infotainment, wheels, etc.)

#### Step 8: SEO Settings

1. Enter a **Meta Title** — The title shown in Google search results (max 60 characters)
2. Enter a **Meta Description** — The description shown in Google (max 160 characters)

#### Step 9: Publishing Options

| Setting | Options | Description |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Status | Available, Reserved, Sold, Coming Soon, In Preparation | Visibility on the website |
| Featured | Yes / No | Show on homepage carousel |

#### Step 10: Save

- Click **"Save & Publish"** to make the vehicle live immediately
- Click **"Save as Draft"** to save and publish later

After saving, you will see a success message and a link to view the vehicle on the live website.

### 2.3 Editing a Vehicle

1. Go to **Vehicles** in the sidebar
2. Find the vehicle you want to edit
3. Click the **Edit** icon (pencil) on the right side
4. Make your changes
5. Click **"Save Changes"**

### 2.4 Duplicating a Vehicle

Duplicating is useful when adding similar vehicles:

1. Find the vehicle you want to copy
2. Click the **More Actions** menu (three dots)
3. Select **"Duplicate"**
4. A copy will be created with status "In Preparation"
5. Edit the copy to change registration, mileage, year, price, and images
6. Save the new vehicle

### 2.5 Deleting a Vehicle

**Warning:** Deletion is permanent and cannot be undone.

1. Find the vehicle in the list
2. Click the **More Actions** menu (three dots)
3. Select **"Delete"**
4. Confirm the deletion in the dialog

**Alternative:** Instead of deleting, change the status to "Sold" — this hides it from the website but keeps the record.

## 3. Managing Inventory

### 3.1 Vehicle Status Management

Vehicle status controls what visitors see on your website:

| Status | Website Display | When to Use |
|--------|----------------|-------------|
| **Available** | Fully visible with price | Vehicle is ready for sale |
| **Reserved** | Visible with "Reserved" badge | Deposit paid, sale pending |
| **Sold** | Hidden from public | Vehicle has been sold |
| **Coming Soon** | Visible with "Coming Soon" badge | Vehicle arriving at showroom |
| **In Preparation** | Hidden from public | Being prepared for sale |

**To change status:**
1. Find the vehicle in the list
2. Click the status badge (or Edit and change the Status dropdown)
3. Select the new status
4. Save

### 3.2 Bulk Actions

Perform actions on multiple vehicles at once:

1. Select vehicles by clicking the checkbox on the left of each row
2. Click the **Bulk Actions** dropdown at the top of the table
3. Choose your action:

| Action | What It Does |
|--------|-------------|
| Change Status | Update status for all selected vehicles |
| Mark as Featured | Add selected vehicles to homepage |
| Remove Featured | Remove from homepage |
| Duplicate | Create copies of all selected vehicles |
| Export CSV | Download vehicle data as a spreadsheet |
| Delete | Remove all selected vehicles |

### 3.3 Filtering and Searching

Use the filter bar to find specific vehicles:

| Filter | How to Use |
|--------|-----------|
| Search | Type any text — searches make, model, and registration |
| Status | Select from dropdown |
| Make | Select manufacturer |
| Fuel Type | Select fuel type |
| Price Range | Enter minimum and maximum price |
| Year Range | Enter minimum and maximum year |

Click **"Clear Filters"** to reset all filters.

## 4. Managing Leads

The Leads section is your CRM (Customer Relationship Management) system for tracking all customer enquiries.

### 4.1 Understanding Lead Types

| Type | Description | Icon |
|------|-------------|------|
| **Contact** | General enquiry about a vehicle or service | Message bubble |
| **Sell My Car** | Customer wants to sell their vehicle to you | Tag |
| **Test Drive** | Customer wants to book a test drive | Calendar |
| **Finance** | Customer applying for vehicle finance | Credit card |

### 4.2 Viewing Leads

Click **Leads** in the sidebar to view all leads. You can switch between:

- **Pipeline View** — Kanban board showing leads organized by status
- **List View** — Sortable table format

### 4.3 Processing a New Lead

When a new lead arrives:

#### Step 1: Acknowledge (within 15 minutes)

1. Click on the lead to open it
2. Read the enquiry details
3. Click **"Acknowledge"** or update status to **"Contacted"**
4. This lets your team know you're handling it

#### Step 2: Make Initial Contact (within 2 hours)

Contact the customer by phone or email:

**By phone:**
1. Call the phone number provided
2. Introduce yourself: "Hello, this is [Your Name] from APEX Automotive. I received your enquiry about..."
3. Ask about their requirements
4. Take notes during the call

**By email:**
1. Click the **"Send Email"** button in the lead detail
2. Choose a template or write your own message
3. Send the email

#### Step 3: Record Your Interaction

1. Scroll to the **Notes** section
2. Click **"Add Note"**
3. Write a summary of your conversation:
   - What the customer is looking for
   - Their budget
   - Their timeline
   - Any specific requirements
   - Next steps agreed
4. Click **"Save Note"**

#### Step 4: Update Status

Based on your conversation, update the lead status:

- **New** → **Contacted**: You've made first contact
- **Contacted** → **Qualified**: You understand their needs and have a suitable vehicle
- **Qualified** → **Appointment**: You've arranged a meeting or test drive
- **Appointment** → **Converted**: They've agreed to purchase
- Any status → **Declined**: They're no longer interested

### 4.4 Assigning Leads

To assign a lead to a team member:

1. Open the lead detail page
2. Click the **Assigned To** field
3. Select a team member from the dropdown
4. The assignee will be notified

### 4.5 Adding Notes

Notes are internal records visible only to staff:

1. Open the lead
2. Scroll to the **Internal Notes** section
3. Type your note
4. Click **"Add Note"**

**Best practices for notes:**
- Be specific and factual
- Record customer preferences and requirements
- Note any commitments you made
- Include dates and times of conversations
- Write clearly — other team members may need to read these

### 4.6 Lead Status Definitions

| Status | Meaning | Typical Actions |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| **New** | Just received, not yet contacted | Acknowledge and plan first contact |
| **Contacted** | Initial contact made | Follow up with information |
| **Qualified** | Needs understood, vehicle identified | Arrange viewing/test drive |
| **Appointment** | Meeting or test drive scheduled | Prepare for appointment |
| **Converted** | Sale completed or committed | Process sale paperwork |
| **Declined** | No longer interested | Record reason, consider re-engagement |

## 5. Managing Test Drives

### 5.1 Viewing Test Drive Bookings

1. Go to **Leads** in the sidebar
2. Click the **Test Drives** tab
3. View bookings in:
   - **Calendar view** — See bookings by day/week/month
   - **List view** — See all bookings as a table

### 5.2 Confirming a Test Drive

1. Open the booking details
2. Check that the requested vehicle is available
3. Check for scheduling conflicts
4. Click **"Confirm"**
5. The customer will receive a confirmation

### 5.3 Day-of-Test-Drive Preparation

Before each test drive:

- [ ] Ensure the vehicle has fuel
- [ ] Clean the vehicle inside and out
- [ ] Move the vehicle to a prominent position
- [ ] Have the keys ready
- [ ] Prepare any paperwork if a purchase is likely
- [ ] Review the customer's requirements and preferences

### 5.4 After the Test Drive

1. Record the outcome in the lead notes
2. Update the lead status if appropriate
3. Follow up with the customer within 24 hours

## 6. Managing Finance Applications

### 6.1 Viewing Finance Applications

1. Go to **Leads** in the sidebar
2. Filter by type: **Finance**
3. Click on an application to view details

### 6.2 Reviewing an Application

The application shows:
- Customer details (name, contact, employment)
- Requested vehicle and amount
- Employment status and income
- Credit rating (self-reported)

### 6.3 Processing Steps

1. **Review** — Check all details are complete
2. **Verify documents** — If supporting documents were uploaded, review them
3. **Credit check** — Run a formal credit check through your finance provider
4. **Make decision:**
   - **Approved** — Contact customer with terms
   - **Declined** — Inform customer with explanation
   - **Documents Required** — Request additional information
5. **Record the decision** with notes

## 7. Managing Blog Posts

### 7.1 Creating a Blog Post

1. Click **Blog** in the sidebar (Marketing team access)
2. Click **"New Post"**
3. Fill in the form:

| Field | What to Enter |
|-------|--------------|
| Title | Compelling headline |
| Category | Select from dropdown |
| Excerpt | 1-2 sentence summary |
| Content | Full article text |
| Featured Image | Upload main image |
| Tags | Keywords separated by commas |
| Meta Title | SEO title (60 chars max) |
| Meta Description | SEO description (160 chars max) |

4. Click **"Save as Draft"** to save without publishing
5. Or click **"Publish"** to make it live immediately

### 7.2 Blog Writing Tips

- Write at least 500 words for best SEO results
- Use headings to structure your content
- Include relevant keywords naturally
- Add a call-to-action at the end ("Browse our inventory," "Contact us")
- Use the featured image to attract attention
- Proofread before publishing

### 7.3 Managing Published Posts

- **Edit:** Click the edit icon to modify a published post
- **Unpublish:** Change status to "Draft" to hide from website
- **Delete:** Permanently remove the post

## 10. Managing Staff

**Note:** This section is for administrators only.

### 10.1 Adding a New User

1. Go to **Settings**
2. Click the **Staff Users** tab
3. Click **"Add User"**
4. Enter:
   - First Name
   - Last Name
   - Email Address
   - Phone Number (optional)
   - Role (see below)
5. Click **"Send Invitation"**
6. The user will receive an email to set their password

### 10.2 User Roles

| Role | What They Can Do |
|------|-----------------|
| **Super Admin** | Everything — full system control |
| **Admin** | Manage vehicles, leads, settings, view analytics |
| **Staff** | Add/edit vehicles, view and update leads |
| **Sales** | Manage leads only |
| **Marketing** | Manage blog posts, view limited analytics |

### 10.3 Deactivating a User

1. Find the user in the Staff Users list
2. Click the **Deactivate** button
3. Confirm the action
4. The user will immediately lose access

**Note:** Deactivation is reversible. To reactivate, click **Activate**.

### 10.4 Changing a User's Role

1. Find the user in the list
2. Click the **Edit** icon
3. Select a new role from the dropdown
4. Click **Save**

## 11. FAQ

### General Questions

**Q: What browsers are supported?**
A: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge (latest 2 versions). Internet Explorer is not supported.

**Q: Can I use the admin on my mobile phone?**
A: Yes, the admin panel is responsive and works on tablets and phones, though a desktop computer is recommended for the best experience.

**Q: How do I change my password?**
A: Click your profile photo → "My Profile" → "Change Password." Enter your current password and your new password twice.

**Q: I forgot my password. What do I do?**
A: Click "Forgot Password" on the login screen. Enter your email address and follow the instructions sent to your inbox.

### Vehicle Questions

**Q: How many images can I upload per vehicle?**
A: Up to 20 images per vehicle.

**Q: What image formats are accepted?**
A: JPG, PNG, and WebP.

**Q: Can I change the order of images?**
A: Yes. Drag and drop images to reorder them.

**Q: What happens when I mark a vehicle as "Sold"?**
A: It is immediately hidden from the public website but remains in your admin system for records.

**Q: Can I recover a deleted vehicle?**
A: No, deletion is permanent. We recommend changing the status to "Sold" instead of deleting.

### Lead Questions

**Q: How do I know when a new lead comes in?**
A: You will receive an in-app notification and an email. The notification bell in the top bar will also show a badge.

**Q: Can I assign a lead to another team member?**
A: Yes. Open the lead, click the "Assigned To" field, and select a team member.

**Q: What does "Qualified" mean?**
A: It means you have spoken with the customer, understand their needs, and have identified a suitable vehicle for them.

**Q: How do I export leads?**
A: Select leads using checkboxes, click "Bulk Actions," and choose "Export CSV."

### Technical Questions

**Q: Is my data secure?**
A: Yes. All data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS) and at rest. We use industry-standard security practices.

**Q: How often is data backed up?**
A: Database backups occur every hour (incremental) and daily (full). Images are backed up daily.

**Q: Who can access my account?**
A: Only you. Never share your login credentials. Administrators can deactivate accounts but cannot see your password.
